Section 1: Introduction

Who is
Facebook Elevate?

Our program was founded in 2018 by a group of intrepid Facebook employees who shared a singular vision: to support local communities of color. Together they launched Elevate with a mission to accelerate the economic success and impact of Black, Latinx and Hispanic communities by providing access to free marketing education, mentorship, and support.

Who is Elevate for?
  • Small businesses
  • Content creators
  • Nonprofits
  • Community leaders
  • Job seekers and students

Section 2: Bring your business online

Create and
publish content

Connect and engage with your customers with various types of content across Facebook and Instagram.

  • Posts

    Post on Facebook and Instagram to share information about new products and upcoming events.

  • Stories

    When you use Stories, you can attract people’s attention with bite-sized content in a vertical fullscreen format with content that disappears in 24 hours. Show an authentic side of your business, tell your business’s story in a creative and unique way and invite your community to engage with you through Stories’ interactive features.

  • Live

    Broadcast live on Facebook and Instagram to stream a conversation, performance, Q&A or virtual event. When broadcasting live, your community has the opportunity to follow along in real time and join the conversation by asking questions, commenting and reacting to your video.

  • Reels

    Reels invites you to create fun videos to share with your friends or anyone on Instagram. Record and edit 15-second multi-clip videos with audio, effects and new creative tools.

  • IGTV

    IGTV is long-form, immersive video. It can be as long as you want, and takes over people’s entire screens. Similar to Stories or photo and video feed posts, you can like, comment and share IGTV videos with friends.

Small Business Tools

Create events on your Page to build community around your business. You can set up free or paid online events for your business, as well as manage who you want to invite, check who has RSVP’d and share messages about your events directly from your Page.

Improve your
creative strategy

Create new assets by building a DIY studio.

How to get started:

Set up your home studio near a natural light source such as a window. Use white paper or poster board to reflect or bounce light if needed.

Leave enough room to crop your video or images for posts (1:1) and Stories (9:16).

Stabilize your mobile device using a tripod. If you don’t have one available, get crafty and use household items such as books. Remove clutter from the frame so your products are the main focus.

Reposition your camera and shoot from different angles and backgrounds.

Establish
business goals

Business goals can help you focus on what you want to accomplish with your marketing efforts and help you keep a record of your progress over time. Advertising can play an important part in helping you achieve your business goals, like reaching more people who are interested in a product or service and prompting them to take action. Make sure to consider your business goal when creating your ad campaign so that you can select the ad objective that’s most relevant to it.

 Start defining your business goals
Section 4: Scale your efforts with advertising

Define your ideal customer

When you advertise, focus on finding the customers that matter most to your business. Define a target audience, which is the group of people most likely to be interested in what a business is offering, before you start advertising to increase the effectiveness of ads and help you reach business goals.

To define a target audience and the ads they see on Facebook, first you’ll need to understand their needs, characteristics and motivations.

Ads Manager overview

Ads Manager is the starting point for running more sophisticated ads across Facebook apps and services. It’s an all-in-one tool for creating ads, managing when and where they’ll run and tracking how well campaigns are performing. Businesses can use Ads Manager to manage ads and see an overview of their efforts across multiple platforms. When advertisers see which elements of their campaigns perform best, they can adjust their ads to maximize their budgets and time to achieve their business goals.

Create a
content calendar

A content calendar can help you stay organized as you connect with customers online. Content calendars come in a variety of formats but typically include five key pieces of information.

Date

When will the post be published?

Theme

What is your post about?

Visual description

Include a sentence or two about how you want your post image or video to look.

Format

Posts can have pictures, video or a combination of the two. Decide which you will create for your post.

Platform

Where do you intend to post the content (Facebook, Instagram, or elsewhere)?
